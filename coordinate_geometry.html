📍 What is Coordinate Geometry? STARTER

Coordinate Geometry is the bridge between algebra and geometry. Instead of describing shapes with words, we place them on a grid and use numbers to pinpoint every position!

René Descartes invented this system in the 1600s — legend says he was watching a fly on his ceiling and wondered how to describe its exact position. Mathematics from bug-watching!

Every point lives on the Cartesian Plane — a flat grid with two number lines crossing at right angles.

The horizontal line is the x-axis and the vertical line is the y-axis. They meet at the Origin (0, 0).

🎯 Plotting Points EASY

A point is written as (x, y). Think of it as a treasure map — x tells you to walk left/right, y tells you to climb up/down.

Point P = (x, y)
x = horizontal distance from origin · y = vertical distance from origin

The Four Quadrants:

Quadrant I: (+, +) 😊
Quadrant II: (−, +) 🌿
Quadrant III: (−, −) 🌙
Quadrant IV: (+, −) 🌸

📏 Distance Formula MEDIUM

How far apart are two points? Use the Distance Formula — it's just Pythagoras's theorem in disguise! 🥷

d = √[ (x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)² ]
Where A=(x₁,y₁) and B=(x₂,y₂)

Example: Distance from A(1, 2) to B(4, 6):

d = √[(4−1)² + (6−2)²] = √[9 + 16] = √25 = 5 units

🎯 Midpoint Formula MEDIUM

The midpoint is the exact middle of a line segment — just average the coordinates!

M = ( (x₁+x₂)/2 , (y₁+y₂)/2 )
The point exactly halfway between A and B

Example: Midpoint of A(2, 4) and B(8, 10):

M = ((2+8)/2, (4+10)/2) = (5, 7)
📈 Slope & Straight Lines ADVANCED

Slope (m) tells us how steep a line is. Think of it as the "rise over run" of a hill 🏔️

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁) = rise / run
Equation of a line: y = mx + c

Where m = slope  |  c = y-intercept (where line crosses y-axis)

Positive slope ↗ (going up)
Negative slope ↘ (going down)
Zero slope → (flat / horizontal)
Undefined slope ↕ (vertical)

📐 Formula Cheat Sheet

📍 Point & Coordinates
P = (x, y)
x = horizontal, y = vertical

Origin = (0, 0)

x-axis: y = 0

y-axis: x = 0

📏 Distance Formula
d = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]
Distance between two points

This is just Pythagoras' theorem!

Distance from origin to (x,y): √(x²+y²)

🎯 Midpoint Formula
M = ((x₁+x₂)/2, (y₁+y₂)/2)
Exact centre of two points
📈 Slope (Gradient)
m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)
Rise ÷ Run

Parallel lines: m₁ = m₂

Perpendicular lines: m₁ × m₂ = −1

📉 Equation of Line
y = mx + c
Slope-intercept form
y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)
Point-slope form
🔺 Section Formula
P = ((mx₂+nx₁)/(m+n), (my₂+ny₁)/(m+n))
Point dividing AB in ratio m:n
